The single most-asked question after a bail bond call: "How much does this actually cost?" The honest answer is straightforward — California fixes the rate by statute. This guide explains the 10% premium, the math at each bail level, payment plan options, and when collateral comes into play.

The 10% Rule — Set by California Law

Under California Insurance Code §1800.4, the bail bond premium is fixed at 10% of the total bail amount for licensed bondsmen. There is no legal way to charge less for the actual fee. Anyone advertising below 10% as the actual rate is either:

  • Operating illegally (unlicensed)
  • Using misleading marketing — the advertised number is the down payment on a payment plan, not the actual premium

The 10% applies whether the case ends in dismissal, plea, conviction, or acquittal. It is non-refundable.

What's Included in the 10%

For reputable California bondsmen, the 10% premium covers:

  • Posting the surety bond with the court or jail
  • The bondsman's financial risk of guaranteeing the defendant's appearance
  • Indemnity agreement preparation
  • 24/7 communication and case management
  • Court compliance tracking

For Angels Bail Bonds, the 10% also includes: pre-qualification of cosigners over the phone, walking cosigners through the indemnity agreement before signing, and tracking defendant transfers between LA County jail facilities.

What's NOT Included (Watch For These)

  • Late fees on payment plan installments — disclosed in writing in the indemnity agreement
  • Recovery agent fees if the defendant fails to appear (typically 10-25% of bail amount)
  • Collateral storage or appraisal fees in some high-value cases
  • Court filing fees for bond exoneration motions

Reputable bondsmen disclose all of these upfront. If a bondsman cannot or will not give you a complete fee schedule in writing, walk away.

Payment Plan Structure

Most California bondsmen offer payment plans for higher premiums. Typical structure:

  • Down payment: 20-50% of total premium at posting
  • Monthly installments: 6-12 months
  • No interest on premium: Per California Department of Insurance regulations
  • Auto-pay enrollment: Most plans require auto-debit from a bank account or credit card
  • Late fees: Disclosed in writing in the indemnity agreement

Example for a $5,000 premium ($50,000 bail): $1,000 down + $400/month × 10 months. Or $2,500 down + $500/month × 5 months. Terms vary by cosigner financial standing.

When Collateral Is Required

For bail amounts of $50,000+ or higher-risk cosigners, the bondsman may require collateral in addition to the cash premium. Common collateral types:

  • Real estate — equity in a primary residence or rental property; recorded as a lien
  • Vehicles — title held until bond exonerated
  • Cash deposit — held in escrow until bond exonerated
  • Jewelry, electronics — physically held by bondsman

Collateral is released back to the cosigner when the defendant completes all court appearances and the bond is exonerated under California Penal Code §1305. This typically takes 3-9 months for misdemeanors, 6 months to 2+ years for felonies.
