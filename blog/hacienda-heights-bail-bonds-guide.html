LASD Industry Station: Where Hacienda Heights Arrests Go

Since Hacienda Heights has no incorporated city government or municipal police force, all arrests are made by LASD deputies and processed at the LASD Industry Station, located at 150 N Hudson Ave, City of Industry, CA 91744 (phone: 626-330-3322).

The Industry Station serves Hacienda Heights, the City of Industry, La Puente, La Habra Heights, and surrounding unincorporated areas of East and West Valinda, Bassett, and North Whittier. It is an LASD patrol station with a holding facility — defendants can be booked and held here for short-term custody while bail is arranged. Defendants facing serious felony charges or requiring longer-term pre-trial detention are transferred to central LASD facilities, most commonly the Men's Central Jail (MCJ) or Pitchess Detention Center.

To locate someone in LASD custody — whether at Industry Station or a central facility — use the LASD Inmate Information Center at app5.lasd.org/iic. Records typically appear 2 to 6 hours after booking is entered into the system.

The Booking Timeline at LASD Industry Station

Booking at a Sheriff's station is a multi-step administrative process that must be completed before bail can be posted. Here is what happens at Industry Station after an arrest in Hacienda Heights:

Step 1
Arrest and Transport
An LASD deputy makes the arrest and transports the defendant to Industry Station at 150 N Hudson Ave. The deputy completes the arrest report — charges, circumstances, identifying information — which initiates the booking process.
Step 2
Personal Property Inventory
All personal items are catalogued, bagged, and secured. A property receipt is issued to the defendant. Phone contact with family typically ends at this step.
Step 3
Fingerprinting (Live Scan)
Electronic fingerprints are submitted to state and federal databases. This step confirms identity and reveals any outstanding warrants from other jurisdictions. An active warrant — even from another county — can hold the defendant even after bail is posted on the current charges.
Step 4
Photograph
A booking photo is taken and linked to the defendant's case record in the LASD database. This is the record that appears in the public inmate locator once processing is complete.
Step 5
Medical Screening
California requires a health screening at every booking. The screening covers injuries, medications, communicable diseases, and mental health needs. Medical issues identified here can add time to the overall booking process.
Step 6
Bail Amount Set
Bail is assigned per the 2026 LA County Felony or Misdemeanor Bail Schedule, published by the Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles, effective January 1, 2026. Schedule amounts are set without a judge for most charges. Cases involving specified serious felonies require magistrate review.
Step 7
Housing or Transfer
The defendant is either held at Industry Station for short-term custody or transferred to a central facility. Bond can be posted at the station or, if the defendant is transferred, at the receiving facility. Total time from arrest to bail eligibility is typically 4 to 12 hours for felony arrests.

2026 LA County Bail Schedule — What to Expect

The Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles publishes the Felony and Misdemeanor Bail Schedules annually, effective January 1. These set the standard bail amount for each charge at booking. A judge at arraignment — required within 48 hours of arrest under California Penal Code § 825 (excluding Sundays and court holidays) — may adjust bail upward or downward based on flight risk, criminal history, and community ties.

Here are representative amounts from the 2026 LA County bail schedule for charges commonly seen in Hacienda Heights and the City of Industry corridor:

Posting bail before arraignment is the best way to lock in the scheduled amount. If arraignment arrives before bail is posted on a serious felony charge, the judge can set a higher amount — or deny bail entirely for charges that qualify.

How a Bail Bond Works in Hacienda Heights

Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, the standard bail bond premium is 10% of the total bail amount. For a $50,000 bail, you pay $5,000 to the bondsman — not $50,000 to the court. The bondsman issues a surety bond to the court guaranteeing the defendant's appearance. The 10% premium is non-refundable once the bond is posted — it is the service fee, not a deposit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is someone taken after an arrest in Hacienda Heights?

All arrests in Hacienda Heights (an unincorporated community) are made by LASD deputies and processed at the LASD Industry Station, 150 N Hudson Ave, City of Industry, CA 91744. Serious felony defendants are later transferred to central LASD facilities such as Men's Central Jail or Pitchess Detention Center.

How long does booking at Industry Station take?

Misdemeanor bookings typically take 4 to 8 hours from arrest to bail eligibility. Felony bookings, or arrests involving outstanding warrants, can take 8 to 16 hours. Weekend and holiday bookings tend toward the longer end of the range.

What does a bail bond cost in Hacienda Heights?

Can bail be posted at LASD Industry Station?

Yes. All LASD facilities, including Industry Station, accept bail bonds 24 hours a day. If the defendant is transferred to a central facility, the bond is posted at that location. Angels Bail Bonds monitors transfers and handles this automatically.

Is the bail bond premium refundable?

No. Under California law, the 10% premium is non-refundable once the bond is posted. It is the service fee for posting the surety bond with the court — not a deposit that gets returned.
